# Claim ledger

Checked: 2026-07-15

This ledger separates canonical claims, later Theravāda systematization, Mahāsi instructions, modern editorial guidance, and safety evidence. A source code identifies a text. It does not make every nearby sentence equally strong.

## Strength scale

- **Direct:** the linked source explicitly states or defines the claim.
- **Strong:** the claim is a close paraphrase, but wording or application requires context.
- **Lineage:** the source documents a Mahāsi or commentarial teaching, not direct speech of the Buddha.
- **Safety evidence:** empirical research relevant to risk management, not Buddhist doctrine.
- **Editorial:** a practical recommendation by this book. It must not be presented as scripture or a guaranteed path result.

## Claims excluded unless stronger evidence is added

- “The Mahāsi method has been verified on millions of meditators.” No reliable denominator or outcome definition is supplied.
- “Most practitioners progress fastest while ill.” No source supports the comparative claim.
- “The canon records attainment specifically at ages 80 and 120.” Exact passages and identity checks are missing.
- “A particular sensation proves a named insight stage.” Phenomenology overlaps with sleep, anxiety, expectation, concentration, and other conditions.
- “A fixed retreat length or daily schedule produces Stream-entry.” The cited sources do not support a guaranteed dose-response claim.
